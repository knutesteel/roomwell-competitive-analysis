  1. Vagaro — SOAP notes behind paywall is a wedge. "Pay extra for so many things including soap notes." Source: BusyBook.
  2. Mindbody — contract lock-in is the biggest landmine. "If this had been mentioned, I would not have proceeded" + "contracts are strictly enforced." Source: Trustpilot.

"The new free plan doesn't support calendar sync, which makes it basically non-functional for someone who doesn't keep regular business hours but is by appointment only, and paying $29 a month just for calendar sync seems absurd." Source: Square Community.
  5. GlossGenius — same SOAP-paywall pattern as Vagaro. "I have to pay for gold to get a SOAP note format; I feel the app is set up more ideally for cosmetology than for massage, unless you pay more." Source: Capterra.

Switching activity

  • Mindbody → Vagaro: at least one solo cited "WAY too many features" as the reason.
  • Vagaro → ???: BusyBook frames a clear exodus narrative ("Why Solo Massage Therapists Are Switching From Vagaro") but doesn't name a dominant destination. Pattern: Vagaro buyers churn to whichever platform gives them SOAP/charting in-base.
  • Mindbody → MassageBook / Acuity / Fresha / GlossGenius: three vendor pages position themselves explicitly as Mindbody alternatives for solo wellness providers.
  • Jane → Noterro: vendor narrative ("so much more user-friendly and intuitive") and price gap ($33 vs $54+ entry) suggest steady switching among SOAP-focused solos.

Real competitive set for Roomwell (baseline): Direction points away from salon-first platforms toward all-in-one solo-LMT tools that include SOAP notes, automated reminders, and online booking in base. Direct competitors: Noterro, ClinicSense, MassageBook. Jane is the upmarket boundary.

Noterro

SOAP-focused solos appreciate "efficiency and ease" that allows them to "devote more time with and on clientele instead of paperwork."Noterro

Acuity

"Acuity helps me look more professional as a solopreneur and improves my efficiency by allowing clients to book whenever they want instead of calling me directly."Brianna Evans, solo LMT — Acuity case study

Reasoning patterns

  • New solos (low client volume) are most price-sensitive and gravitate to free or sub-$30 tiers.
  • Mid-stage solos (steady book) prioritize SOAP/charting, automated reminders, and intake — and resent paying extra for any of those.
  • Late-stage solos / clinic-curious operators reach for Jane, but at the price tier accept clinical-grade complexity.
