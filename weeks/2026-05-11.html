Cost complaints

Mindbody

"Bait and switch company taking advantage of small businesses with little to no transparency about their program without actually signing up and paying ridiculous fees. Then will not allow you to cancel (one day later) when you finally enter the program... Do not sign any kind of contract with them because you will absolutely not be able to get out."BBB
"Businesses are expected to sign a 12-month contract and commit $2k – $7k of their revenue to Mindbody."Pabau pricing analysis — first concrete commitment-floor range we've captured. Monthly plans range $139 (Starter) to $599 (Ultimate Plus).

Fresha

"I'm being charged for customers who scan my in-store QR code to book."Massage therapist, Capterra. Same trust-erosion pattern as MassageBook directory fees.

Jane

"At $54-139/month, it's expensive for what is essentially scheduling with clinical add-ons. If you don't need charting or insurance billing, a more affordable scheduling platform will serve you better."EHR Insider. The reviewer is essentially writing Roomwell's segmentation in plain English.

Noterro

"Cost was far too high while trying to start a business and didn't like that essential features would require them to increase their subscription."Capterra. First Noterro cost complaint we've captured — tier-upgrade creep exists at Noterro too.

Vagaro

"If you cancel your account or deactivate credit card processing within 12 months of ordering the card reader, you will be charged the price of the reader."Vagaro Support. Official policy explaining last week's $140 "free card reader" clawback complaints.

Square Appointments

"Calendar sync would cost $40 per month as part of the Plus plan — a significant expense for solo operators who only need this specific feature."Square Community. Same complaint as last week's thread, surfaced in a separate location — pattern is widespread.

No new findings this week for Acuity, MassageBook, ClinicSense, or Schedulicity in the cost theme.

Switching activity

The dominant switching story this week is attempted switching that Mindbody is blocking via contract enforcement. BBB complaints describe customers locked into contracts "through September 11, 2026" even after one-day cancellation attempts. Less "where they're switching to," more "where they're stuck."

Notable: solos churning off Jane (charting overhead) don't name a destination. And solos coming onto Jane from informal tools (Google Calendar + paper) bypass MassageBook/ClinicSense entirely. Worth investigating whether that's SEO, vendor reputation, or word-of-mouth.

Competitive set, refined

  • Upmarket boundary: Jane (validated by EHR Insider's "more affordable scheduling platform will serve you better")
  • Direct competitors: Noterro, ClinicSense, MassageBook — cracks emerging this week (ClinicSense mobile, ClinicSense + Noterro support quality)
  • Indirect threat: Fresha — free entry tier pulls solos in, then marketplace fees create MassageBook-style trust friction
