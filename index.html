Weekly listening sweep of what solo massage therapists are saying about the major booking and practice-management platforms.

Latest run: 2026-05-18 Platforms tracked: 9 Findings this week: 22

Roomwell opportunity summary

Across three weeks of listening, one gap keeps showing up — and now a first direct competitor has arrived to prove it's real. The underserved middle of massage practice-management software is confirmed: solo LMTs are priced out and overwhelmed by clinical-grade tools like Jane, yet frustrated by salon-first platforms — Vagaro, Mindbody, Fresha, Square — that gate massage-specific features behind add-ons and lock practitioners into contracts. BusyBook for Massage launched March 2026 targeting this exact gap — the first named direct competitor. Roomwell's opening remains real, but the race has started. Key wedges: SOAP notes and booking included in base price, no contracts, strong mobile, charting that works out of the box, and a calm/craft product voice that no current competitor is using.

Opportunity

No contracts, transparent pricing

Mindbody's 12-month lock-in is the single most-quoted grievance — "you will absolutely not be able to get out," with $2k–$7k commitments. Vagaro's "free card reader" clawback and add-on creep reinforce it. Month-to-month with no surprises is a positioning wedge. BusyBook leads with "no contracts" too — this is now a table-stakes expectation, not a differentiator alone.

Sources: BBB, Pabau, Capterra

Opportunity

Massage features in base, not paywalled

SOAP notes sit behind paywalls at Vagaro and GlossGenius; Fresha was "designed for hairdressers rather than massage therapists." Schedulicity "does lack the ability to include soap notes" entirely. Solos want SOAP, booking, and reminders in the base price — not assembled from add-ons.

Sources: BusyBook, Capterra, G2

Opportunity

Simple charting, not template-engineering

Jane churns non-clinical solos who get "easily overwhelmed" by charting setup — "the biggest downside is the setting up of charting options." Opinionated, ready-to-use SOAP templates win this segment. BusyBook is also targeting this with AI-assisted notes.

Source: Capterra

Opportunity

Mobile-first, for working between sessions

Both ClinicSense ("the phone app just feels choppy") and MassageBook ("intakes... only desktop") have named mobile weaknesses. MassageBook's own competitor page now calls ClinicSense "not mobile-friendly." A strong mobile-first experience is a defensible product wedge.

Sources: Capterra, MassageBook

New opportunity

Reliability as revenue protection

Week 3 surfaced a Vagaro pain point beyond cost: booking instability causing documented revenue loss. "This happened frequently enough that I questioned if I was losing new business." Reliability-as-revenue-protection is a positioning angle no current competitor leads with.

Source: Capterra Dec 2025

New opportunity

Google Business integration gap

Vagaro's lack of Google Business / Reserve with Google integration is a discoverability problem for solos dependent on local search. "The monthly payments for an app that doesn't integrate with Google Business is way too high." No competitor content addresses this angle yet.

Source: Capterra
