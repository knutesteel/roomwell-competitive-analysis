Trends vs. previous report

Intensifying

Mindbody contract lock-in

BBB complaints surfaced this week with sharper language: "bait and switch," "prey on a new small business," "active contract through September 11, 2026." Pabau quantifies the commitment at $2k–$7k over 12 months.

Intensifying

Acuity weakness shifts from product to support

Last week's "stagnant product" complaint is showing up this week as "non-existent customer support." Same root cause (Squarespace ownership), different surface.

New

Mobile-app gap at ClinicSense and MassageBook

Mobile complaints surface explicitly at two of three direct competitors this week. Strong mobile-first experience for solo LMTs working between sessions is a defensible product wedge.

New

Fresha as indirect threat

QR-code marketplace fee pattern is the same trust-friction as MassageBook directory fees, but Fresha is faster-growing with a stronger consumer brand. Promote to watch list.

New

First cracks at ClinicSense and Noterro

Both had uniformly positive support signals last week. This week: "service has taken a huge dive" (ClinicSense) and "they say they'll work on it but never do" (Noterro). Small sample — track closely.

The competitive map (refined this week)

  • Upmarket boundary: Jane. Validated again this week by the EHR Insider reviewer who called Jane "expensive for what is essentially scheduling with clinical add-ons."
  • Direct competitors: Noterro, ClinicSense, MassageBook. New cracks this week — ClinicSense mobile-app weakness and a first negative support signal; Noterro picked up its first cost and support complaints.
  • Salon-first platforms (Roomwell isn't competing on their turf): Vagaro, Mindbody, Fresha, Square Appointments. Their solo-LMT customers are the churn pool to target.
  • Stagnant/declining: Acuity (under Squarespace), Schedulicity. Captive but unhappy users.
