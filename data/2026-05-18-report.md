## Switching activity

### BusyBook: New Direct Competitor — Most Important Development This Week

BusyBook (busybook.co) launched "BusyBook for Massage" in March 2026, explicitly targeting solo massage therapists as the primary ICP. Their SEO content is already ranking #1 for "Vagaro alternative massage therapist." Product positioning mirrors Roomwell's target opportunity almost exactly:
- **Pricing:** Free / $20 / $100/mo (no contracts, no add-ons)
- **Differentiation:** AI-powered SOAP notes, AI front desk (client communication via text 24/7), massage-first scheduling
- **Target audience:** Solo practitioners explicitly — "not adapted from a salon platform"
- **Key page:** [busybook.co/blog/vagaro-alternative-massage-therapists/](https://busybook.co/blog/vagaro-alternative-massage-therapists/)

BusyBook is the first named, funded competitor operating in Roomwell's exact product space. Their Starter tier ($20/mo) prices directly against ClinicSense and MassageBook. Their Pro tier ($100/mo) with AI front desk is a clear differentiator angle. Roomwell needs to track their feature velocity and SEO content closely from this point forward.

### Vagaro leavers going to Mangomint (new pattern)
Two separate Capterra reviewers this week name Mangomint as their destination after leaving Vagaro:
- Valentina A. (spa owner): "The constant bugs with the platform made us eventually switch to Mangomint which we have been very happy with!" — Dec 2025. [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/153752/Vagaro/reviews/) (follow-up)
- John G. (owner/CFO): Left Vagaro after one month for Mangomint — Dec 2025. [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/153752/Vagaro/reviews/) (follow-up)

Mangomint starts at ~$165/mo — this is a real upmarket cohort within Vagaro. These are multi-provider businesses, not necessarily Roomwell's solo-LMT ICP. Worth noting the destination as it tells us what "fed up with bugs and overcharging" looks like at the higher end.
