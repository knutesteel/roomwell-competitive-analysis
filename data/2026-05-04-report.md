## This week's highlights

The five most useful findings for product/positioning work:

1. **Vagaro — SOAP notes behind paywall is a wedge.** "pay extra for so many things including soap notes" — solo LMT lamenting that SOAP notes aren't in base plan. Confirms a recurring pattern: salon-first platforms ship clinical features as add-ons, frustrating solo LMTs. Source: [BusyBook](https://busybook.co/blog/vagaro-alternative-massage-therapists/).

2. **Mindbody — contract lock-in is the biggest landmine.** "If this had been mentioned, I would not have proceeded" + "contracts are strictly enforced" — small business signed in Nov 2025, learned about 12-month obligation only at cancellation. Source: [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/mindbodyonline.com).

3. **Acuity — product stagnation under Squarespace is a stated grievance.** "Squarespace acquired Acuity in 2021 and has not added invoicing, contracts, project management, or any business tool beyond scheduling in over four years." If Roomwell can land scheduling + intake + payments natively, that's the gap to attack. Source: [Plutio Freelancer Magazine](https://www.plutio.com/freelancer-magazine/still-using-acuity).

4. **Square Appointments — free-plan downgrade frustrating by-appointment solos.** "the new free plan doesn't support calendar sync, which makes it basically non-functional for someone who doesn't keep regular business hours but is by appointment only, and paying $29 a month just for calendar sync seems absurd." Source: [Square Community](https://community.squareup.com/t5/Beauty-Wellness/Not-happy-about-New-Square-pricing/m-p/357708).

5. **GlossGenius — same SOAP-paywall pattern as Vagaro.** "I have to pay for gold to get a SOAP note format; I feel the app is set up more ideally for cosmetology than for massage, unless you pay more." Two of the four cheaper competitors (Vagaro, GlossGenius) share this exact friction. Source: [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/174830/GlossGenius/reviews/).

## Switching activity

**Direction of flow this week (where signal exists):**

- **Mindbody → Vagaro** — at least one solo cited "WAY too many features" as the reason for the switch ([Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/40229/MINDBODY/reviews/)). More than 50% of Glofox's customers also migrated from Mindbody (industry-wide signal, [Glofox migration guide](https://support.glofox.com/hc/en-us/articles/360004810877-Migrating-from-Mindbody-and-Other-Software-to-Glofox)).
- **Vagaro → ???** — BusyBook's article frames a clear exodus narrative for solo LMTs ("Why Solo Massage Therapists Are Switching From Vagaro") but doesn't name a single dominant destination. Pattern: Vagaro buyers churn to whichever platform gives them SOAP/charting in-base. Source: [BusyBook](https://busybook.co/blog/vagaro-alternative-massage-therapists/).
- **Mindbody → MassageBook / Acuity / Fresha / GlossGenius** — three vendor pages position themselves explicitly as Mindbody alternatives for solo wellness providers; multiple sources tag Mindbody as "overwhelming" for solo. Sources: [MassageBook](https://pro.massagebook.com/alternatives/mindbody), [Acuity](https://acuityscheduling.com/learn/mindbody-alternatives).
- **Jane → Noterro** — vendor narrative ("so much more user-friendly and intuitive," [Software Suggest](https://www.softwaresuggest.com/compare/noterro-vs-jane-app)) and price gap ($33 vs $54+ entry) suggest steady switching among SOAP-focused solos who balk at Jane's clinical-grade pricing.

**Real competitive set for Roomwell, based on this week's signal:**
The destination pattern points away from spa/salon-first platforms (Vagaro, Mindbody, Square) and toward all-in-one solo-LMT tools that include SOAP notes, automated reminders, and online booking *in base*. The clearest direct competitors are **Noterro, ClinicSense, and MassageBook**. Jane is the upmarket boundary — too expensive, too clinical for the solo LMT segment Roomwell is targeting. GlossGenius is the cosmetology-adjacent alternative that *almost* fits but paywall-gates SOAP notes the same way Vagaro does.

## Solo practitioner advice threads

The strongest verbatim recommendations this week:

**ClinicSense (most positive solo signal):**
- "ClinicSense gives me a space to do everything I need, from a seamless intake process, to thorough client notes, to easy billing and gift certificate sales" — solo LMT, [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/178722/ClinicSense/reviews/)
- "the value for money is just right since I only have a few clients right now. I've had excellent customer support" — new sole proprietor, [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/178722/ClinicSense/reviews/)

**Noterro:**
- Quoted in vendor comparison: SOAP-focused solos appreciate "efficiency and ease" that allows them to "devote more time with and on clientele instead of paperwork" — [Noterro](https://www.noterro.com/compare/noterro-vs-jane).

**Acuity:**
- "Acuity helps me look more professional as a solopreneur and improves my efficiency by allowing clients to book whenever they want instead of calling me directly" — Brianna Evans, solo LMT, [Acuity case study](https://acuityscheduling.com/learn/vital-massage-booking-software-case-study). (Vendor-published, but verbatim.)

**Square Appointments:**
- Recommended for solos who want $0/month entry, but the calendar-sync downgrade is breaking trust with by-appointment LMTs.

**Reasoning patterns to note:**
- New solos (low client volume) are most price-sensitive and gravitate to free or sub-$30 tiers.
- Mid-stage solos (steady book) prioritize SOAP/charting, automated reminders, and intake — and resent paying extra for any of those.
- Late-stage solos / clinic-curious operators reach for Jane, but at the price tier accept clinical-grade complexity.
